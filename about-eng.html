I was born in 1992 in Oporto, the same city where I recently completed my studies in Architecture at FAUP with the thesis entitled “Reactivating neglected spaces - punctual interventions as a catalyst for urban regeneration". In the last 7 years, I did several activities to improve my knowledge, such as workshops, summer schools, short courses and assisted several conferences.

I consider myself a sympathetic and open-minded person who likes new challenges. I am a hard-working person who fully commits to all tasks and a fast learner.

After one semester in Gdansk as part of the Erasmus program, I worked 5 months at JvantSpijker, in Rotterdam. As it was a small office, I gained a lot of experience by working in several stages of a project.

 

I just completed my Masters in Architecture, at Oporto School - FAUP, with a thesis that allowed me to focused my research on social, political, economic and environmental aspects that take part of our cities conditioned by the economic crises that has affected the real state market and the planning of cities. The thesis focuses, thus, on bottom-up strategies to intervene in the city that can respond more effectively and quickly to urban and social needs. In this way, we intend to focus on punctual acts/interventions in the city that are of quick response, awake the regeneration of the surroundings and that interfere, in a positive way, in the social dynamics.
