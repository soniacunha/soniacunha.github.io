Summer Pavilion

 

This project was elaborated for an Arquideas contest in 2016. The objective of the contest was to create, on a moderate scale, a multi-usage area in the emblematic Central Park that could host different activities in summer.

This project wants to have the least visual impact on the landscape and be integrated in the natural environment of the park. In this way, mirrors spread around the perimeter allows it to blend between the trees. The white interiors and the “tower” are the only elements revealing that there is something hidden there. The enclosed space hosts a large local rock, giving meaning and an anchor to the pavilion’s site.

 

The pavilion can sustain a lot of different activities with a multi-purpose room, an exhibition room, a small theatre and a restaurant capable of handling small concerts. It is flexible enough to adapt to a wide variety of activities.

Sustainability is a key factor in the project. In addition to not needing any serious preparation of the site, wood was chosen as a building material for its renewability. As an extra, the mirrors on the façade help keeping the heat out, saving energy on the climatization so much needed in the summer.

This place is a cultural and leisure reference point for inhabitants and visitors of the city.
